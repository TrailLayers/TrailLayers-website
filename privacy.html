Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 16, 2026

TrailLayers is an iOS app for tracking outdoor gear, building outfits, and logging activity performance. This policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, when we share it, and how you can control it.

What we collect

  • Account information — such as your email address, display name, and username, which are used to create, authenticate, and manage your account.
  • User-created content — garments, outfits, activity logs, comfort ratings, weather notes, gear details, sizing, retailer details, and other information you choose to enter.
  • Photos — garment or gear images you choose to upload or capture in the app, which are associated with your account. These photos may include people, faces, backgrounds, labels, receipts, or other information visible in the image.
  • Activity and location information — activity details you enter, and optional location information you choose to add to a log using current location or search, such as a place name, trail, city, route area, or coordinates. TrailLayers may collect precise location for this feature even if the app displays only city-level information back to you. If TrailLayers requests device location access, the app will ask for permission first.
  • Technical information — limited information needed to operate, secure, debug, and improve the app, such as device, app, request, error, and security log data.

What we do not collect

  • No advertising identifiers
  • No background location unless a future feature asks for your permission
  • No data broker profiles
  • No broad access to your camera roll beyond the photos you choose to upload
  • No sensitive photos unless you choose to upload or capture them

How your data is stored

All data is stored on Supabase, a hosted database platform. Your content is scoped to your account and is not accessible to other users. Garment images are stored in Supabase Storage. Supabase's infrastructure is hosted on AWS.

How your data is used

  • To provide core TrailLayers features, including wardrobe tracking, outfit building, activity logs, weather and location context, photo classification, and personalized gear insights.
  • To maintain, secure, debug, and improve the app.
  • To respond to support requests and account questions.
  • To develop aggregated or de-identified insights about gear, conditions, activities, and product usage.
  • To personalize recommendations, including gear suggestions, affiliate offers, sponsored gear, or advertising if those features are added in the future.

Photos and AI classification

TrailLayers uses paid Google Gemini or Google Cloud computer vision and AI services to classify garment photos, extract gear attributes, suggest categories, and improve the accuracy of gear records. Garment photos are sent to Google for classification after upload, along with related prompts or metadata needed to process the image.

Google states that data submitted through paid Gemini API services is not used to improve Google products. Google may still process submitted content to provide the service, maintain safety and security, debug issues, prevent abuse, and comply with applicable terms and law. We may store classification results, extracted attributes, prompts, responses, confidence information, or error details to improve accuracy, debug problems, and support the app.

Do not upload photos that contain sensitive personal information unless you are comfortable processing them through TrailLayers. We do not use your photos to train our own AI models without additional notice or consent.

Weather and connected services

Today, weather information may be entered manually. If automated weather features are added, TrailLayers may use activity time and location information to request weather conditions from weather, map, geocoding, or location service providers. If you choose to connect services such as Garmin, Strava, or similar platforms in the future, TrailLayers may import the information you authorize, such as activity start time, duration, distance, and related activity context.

How we share information

We share information with service providers that help us operate TrailLayers, such as hosting, storage, authentication, support, analytics, security, and AI processing providers. We choose service providers and configurations intended to limit their use of personal information to the services they provide to us.

We do not sell personal information that identifies you. We may use or share aggregated or de-identified information for product improvement, research, analytics, gear and activity insights, partnerships, or other business purposes, provided it is not intended to identify you personally.

If we ever want to share identifiable personal information with a brand, retailer, data partner, or advertiser for their own independent use, we will provide additional notice and, where required, ask for your consent.

Analytics and tracking

TrailLayers may use privacy-conscious analytics, diagnostics, or crash reporting to understand app performance, feature usage, and reliability. We do not use advertising identifiers and do not track you across other companies' apps or websites for targeted advertising.

Affiliate links, sponsored content, and advertising

TrailLayers does not currently process payments, show third-party ads, or use hidden tracking pixels. If affiliate links, sponsored gear, paid recommendations, subscriptions, or advertising are added, we may use information in your account to personalize what you see in TrailLayers. If those features involve sharing identifiable personal information with brands, retailers, advertisers, or attribution partners for their own use, we will provide additional notice and, where required, ask for your consent or provide an opt-out.

Deleting your data

You can delete all your data or your entire account at any time from within the app under Profile → Account → Delete All Data or Delete Account. Deleting your account removes your account content from active systems. Some information may remain for a limited time in backups, security logs, or records we must keep for legal, fraud prevention, dispute, or operational reasons.

Your choices

  • You can choose what garments, photos, activity details, and location information you add to TrailLayers.
  • You can revoke device permissions, such as location or photo access, in iOS Settings.
  • You can request access, correction, deletion, or export of your data by contacting us.

Children's privacy

TrailLayers is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect data from children under 13. TrailLayers is also not intended for users under 18 where AI provider terms, connected-service terms, or applicable law restrict access to AI-powered features. If we learn that a child has provided personal information, we will take reasonable steps to delete it.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes materially, the updated date at the top of this page will reflect that. Continued use of the app after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

Contact
